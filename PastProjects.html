Past Projects

Personal, work-related, collaborative and possibly school projects that have been completed.

Objective

Mobile Android Application - This application purpose was to allow customers to create, customize and save various clothing merchandise under your account on your phone. The user would be presented a blank clothing item that they can switch between both sexes and design on all parts of the item. The application came with preset images and colors that could be used on the merchandise and be saved throughout the process of editing and come back to afterwards if not fully satisfied. Users could upload images from the Internet or from their phone storage and plaster them on their item with touch functionality to resize and place in the right position. The user could also download the item they created to use for later use and reorder that same design if needed. Once done designing, users can review their work, pick a brand of clothing, order multiple items and payout using PayPal or their credit/debit cards.

Role

On this project, I was the Team Leader/Coordinator and Tester. I would be in touch with the client to know what he wanted to be built for the application. I made sure the team would be on time with their work and finish their responsibilties. I assigned the documentation to all the members to be completed, created the presentation and showed off the product. I also worked on the PayPal checkout and Google+ and Facebook Login Authentication using their RESTful APIs and did the interface layout for the application using Android Studio.

Timeline

This project went from December 2015 to August 2016.

Technologies

We used Android Studio to build the application, MySQL for our data storing, Google Drive for version control and holding our documents.

Lessons Learned

Pros: Got some expereince with UI/UX and RESTful APIs, saw my leader and presenting skills come into play, used Agile methodologies and XP (Extreme Programming) tactics, and got to work with a real client face-to-face

Cons: A lot of miscommunications between members and client, unrealistic requirements from client, bad version control, application deadline extended, bad scheduling
